Brian Abelson writes code words music jokes in NYC.
As a statistician and developer, Brian has worked for the New York Times, Vox Media, Columbia University, the Gates Foundation, DataKind, and Enigma Technologies. His work centers on the intelligent, responsible, and ethical analysis of data and draws upon his varied skills and experiences in distributed computing, application development, machine learning, geospatial analysis, and visualization. He has degrees in Politics from Whitman College and Applied Statistics from Columbia University.

As a journalist and social scientist, Brian constructs narratives which interweave technical analysis, investigative reporting, and critical interpretation. He's researched racial profiling in New York City, developed novel statistical methods for estimating poverty and audience engagement, surveyed the field of media impact measurement, and investigated the CIA's use of private planes. His work has been published by the Tow Center, Big Data, Open News Source, Verge, and the Daily Beast, and written about in the New York Times, Reuters, BuzzFeed, FastCompany, and Nieman Lab.

Brian DJs and produces under See Other and hosts a bi-weekly radio show and monthly party with Distant Signal. His debut EP came out on Lobster Theremin in 2017. In the past he's remixed Daft Punk, reached number one on Hype Machine, and performed in front of a Cheesecake Factory.

A casual agitator and inveterate smart-ass, Brian occasionally enjoys poking fun at things on the Internet. The New York Times and Urban Outfitters have threatened to sue him.
